Term: Nondramatic Performance
Glossary: music_rights_licensing
Definition: A public performance of a musical composition that does not involve the telling of a story — such as a song played on radio, background music in a movie or TV program, or a live performance in a bar, restaurant, hotel, or store.
Usage Context: ASCAP members grant ASCAP only the right to license nondramatic performances of their copyrighted musical works. An ASCAP license does not authorize dramatic performances. A medley of songs from different Broadway musicals in a cabaret show is generally considered a nondramatic performance.
Source: ASCAP Common Licensing Terms Defined — https://www.ascap.com/help/ascap-licensing/licensing-terms-defined
