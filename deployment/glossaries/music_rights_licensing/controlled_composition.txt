Term: Controlled Composition
Glossary: music_rights_licensing
Definition: A musical composition that is written, owned, or controlled by the recording artist performing it (or an affiliated publisher). Record label contracts often require that controlled compositions be licensed at a reduced mechanical rate (typically 75% of the statutory rate), capped at a maximum number of songs per album.
Usage Context: Standard term in major-label recording agreements that limits the label's mechanical royalty exposure for artist-written material. Controlled composition clauses are a significant point of negotiation between artists and labels.
Source: NMPA — https://www.nmpa.org
