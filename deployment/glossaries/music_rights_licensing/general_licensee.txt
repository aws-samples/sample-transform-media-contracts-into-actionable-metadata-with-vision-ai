Term: General Licensee
Glossary: music_rights_licensing
Definition: An umbrella term referring to the hundreds of thousands of bars, restaurants, hotels, ice and roller skating rinks, theme parks, and other 'brick and mortar' businesses licensed by ASCAP — distinguished from TV, radio, or audio and audiovisual streaming platforms.
Usage Context: Multiple ASCAP license types are available for different categories of general licensees. Businesses playing music for customers typically need both ASCAP and BMI (and often SESAC/GMR) licenses to cover the full repertory.
Source: ASCAP Why ASCAP Licenses Bars, Restaurants and Music Venues — https://www.ascap.com/help/ascap-licensing/why-ascap-licenses-bars-restaurants-music-venues
