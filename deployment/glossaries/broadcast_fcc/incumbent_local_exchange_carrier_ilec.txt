Term: Incumbent Local Exchange Carrier (ILEC)
Glossary: broadcast_fcc
Definition: The local exchange carrier that was providing service in a given area as of the enactment of the Telecommunications Act of 1996 — typically a successor to the regional Bell Operating Companies or independent telephone companies. Subject to specific unbundling and interconnection obligations.
Usage Context: Distinguished from CLECs (Competitive Local Exchange Carriers). ILEC obligations to unbundle network elements and provide interconnection underpin local telecom competition framework.
Source: FCC Telecommunications Glossary — https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DOC-247863A2.pdf
