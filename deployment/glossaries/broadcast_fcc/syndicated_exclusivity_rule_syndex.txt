Term: Syndicated Exclusivity Rule (SYNDEX)
Glossary: broadcast_fcc
Definition: FCC rule allowing a local broadcast station to demand that cable systems in the station's market black out duplicate programming carried on distant signals, when the local station has syndication exclusivity for that program in its market.
Usage Context: Protects local broadcaster investments in syndicated programming. Cable systems must implement blackouts or obtain blanket waivers. Works alongside network non-duplication rules.
Source: FCC Telecommunications Glossary — https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DOC-247863A2.pdf
