Term: Competitive Local Exchange Carrier (CLEC)
Glossary: broadcast_fcc
Definition: A local exchange carrier that competes with the established incumbent local exchange carrier (ILEC) in a given area. CLECs emerged after the Telecommunications Act of 1996 opened local markets to competition.
Usage Context: CLECs may lease unbundled network elements from ILECs or build their own networks. FCC rules govern the rates and terms for interconnection and unbundling.
Source: FCC Telecommunications Glossary — https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DOC-247863A2.pdf
