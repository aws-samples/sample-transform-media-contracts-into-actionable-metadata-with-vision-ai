Term: Direct Broadcast Satellite (DBS)
Glossary: broadcast_fcc
Definition: A high-powered satellite that transmits or retransmits signals intended for direct reception by subscribers. The signal is transmitted to a small earth station or dish (typically around 18 inches in diameter) mounted on homes or other buildings.
Usage Context: DIRECTV and Dish Network are principal U.S. DBS providers. Distribution agreements with DBS providers are distinct from cable and terrestrial broadcast licenses, subject to specific FCC rules.
Source: FCC Telecommunications Glossary — https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DOC-247863A2.pdf
