Term: Subsidiary Rights
Glossary: publishing_literary_rights
Definition: Rights to exploit the work in secondary markets beyond the primary publication format, including book club, first serial (pre-publication magazine/newspaper excerpts), second serial (post-publication), foreign language, dramatic (film, television, stage), audio, commercial/merchandising, and electronic rights. Revenue from subsidiary rights licensed by the publisher is typically shared with the author according to a defined split (e.g., 50/50, 75/25 author-favorable, etc.).
Usage Context: Each subsidiary right has standard industry splits, but these are negotiable. Authors represented by agents often reserve dramatic and first serial rights to the author for the agent to license separately. Authors Guild advises careful attention to each category's split.
Source: Authors Guild Model Trade Book Contract — https://authorsguild.org/resource/model-trade-book-contract/
