Term: Publisher Right to Legal Review
Glossary: publishing_literary_rights
Definition: A clause allowing the publisher to conduct a pre-publication legal review of the manuscript and require changes the publisher deems necessary to reduce legal risk. Authors should ensure the author has meaningful input on any demanded changes.
Usage Context: Authors Guild advocates for author consent rights over legal-review-driven changes, and for a process of consultation rather than unilateral publisher changes. Changes should be documented in writing with rationale provided.
Source: Authors Guild Model Trade Book Contract — https://authorsguild.org/resource/model-trade-book-contract/
