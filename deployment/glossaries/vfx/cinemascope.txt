Term: Cinemascope
Glossary: vfx
Definition: An anamorphic film format that produces an image with an aspect ratio of 2.35: 1. Although Cinemascope (or CinemaScope) was originally a specific process developed by Twentieth Century Fox in the 1950s, it has become a generic term for the 2.35 anamorphic format. The most common lenses used for this purpose today are produced by Panavision.
