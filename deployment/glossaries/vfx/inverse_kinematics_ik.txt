Term: Inverse Kinematics (IK)
Glossary: vfx
Definition: The moving of any part of the skeleton and allowing the software to calculate the joint angles to provide that result. This makes it easier for animators to realistically pose a character, e.g., moving the hand causes the wrist, elbow, and shoulder joints to rotate as necessary to place the hand in that position.
